05 — Reference

Industry Glossary

Key terms used across FIS's platform, products, and the financial technology industry. Click any term to expand its definition.

ACH
Automated Clearing House — US batch payment system for direct deposits, bill payments, B2B transfers.
API Gateway
Entry point that manages, secures, and routes API calls to backend services.
Acquiring
Merchant-side payment processing. FIS exited this space via the Worldpay divestiture (2024).
BaaS (Banking as a Service)
Enabling non-banks to offer banking products via APIs. The FIS platform enables BaaS through Code Connect and modular services.
Buy Side
Investment firms that buy securities: asset managers, hedge funds, PE firms. FIS serves buy-side clients through the Capital Markets segment.
Componentized Architecture
Breaking monolithic systems into independent, reusable modules that can be deployed separately. Enables FIS's incremental modernization approach.
Core Banking
Central system maintaining customer accounts, deposits, loans. The "system of record." FIS's core products include IBS, Horizon, and the Modern Banking Platform.
Core-Agnostic
Platform works regardless of which core system (FIS or non-FIS) processes underneath. A key FIS differentiator allowing adoption without core replacement.
Coreless Banking
Operating without a traditional core system, using a universal ledger instead. Designed for neobanks and digital-only financial institutions.
DORA
Digital Operational Resilience Act — EU regulation for ICT risk management in financial services. FIS is designated as a Critical Third-Party Provider under DORA.
EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer)
Electronic movement of money between accounts, including ATM, POS, and network transactions. FIS operates the NYCE network for EFT.
FFIEC
Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council — US regulator that examines FIS as a technology service provider to banks.
Front-to-Back
Complete coverage of a process from customer-facing (front) through middle-office to back-office operations. FIS's Capital Markets solutions offer front-to-back trade lifecycle coverage.
Issuer Processing
Technology that enables banks (issuers) to issue and process credit/debit cards. FIS Total Issuing Solutions is one of the world's largest issuer processing platforms.
KYC / AML
Know Your Customer / Anti-Money Laundering — regulatory compliance for identity verification and suspicious activity detection.
Ledger
The foundational record-keeping system that tracks all debits, credits, and balances. FIS's Universal Ledger underpins all platform products.
Open Banking
Third-party access to banking data/services via APIs. Code Connect is FIS's open banking enabler and API marketplace.
Orchestration Layer
Middleware that coordinates multiple services/systems to complete a business process. L2 of the FIS platform stack.
Payment Rails
Infrastructure/networks over which payments travel: ACH, wire, instant payments, card networks. FIS Money Movement Hub connects to all major rails via a single API.
Real-Time Payments (RTP)
Instant payment systems that clear and settle immediately. Supported by FIS Money Movement Hub launched May 2025.
Reconciliation
Matching transaction records across systems to ensure consistency and accuracy. Core function in FIS Capital Markets product suite.
Sell Side
Firms that sell/create securities: broker-dealers, investment banks. FIS Cross-Asset Trading Platform serves sell-side market makers and trading firms.
SWIFT
Global messaging network for interbank financial transactions. FIS is a top-5 SWIFT partner, providing connectivity through the Treasury & Risk Suite.
Transfer Agency
Service that maintains records of mutual fund shareholders and processes purchases/redemptions. Part of FIS Trading & Asset Services.

07 — Strategic Moves

Recent Strategic Moves

Key strategic events shaping FIS's transformation from a broad-based fintech to a focused banking and capital markets platform.

2024
Divestiture

Worldpay 55% Sale

FIS sold 55% of Worldpay merchant acquiring business. Remaining 45% later sold to Global Payments in Jan 2026 for $6.6B. FIS exits merchant acquiring entirely to focus on issuing and banking technology.

April 2025
Acquisition Announced

Issuer Solutions Acquisition Announced

FIS announces agreement to acquire Global Payments' Issuer Solutions business (formerly TSYS) for $13.5B ($12B net). The deal adds 40B+ transactions/year, 150+ FI partners, and 75+ country presence in credit processing.

May 2025
Product Launch

Money Movement Hub Launch

FIS launches cloud-native (AWS) Money Movement Hub — a single API for instant payments, ACH, and wire transfers. Core-agnostic, pay-as-you-grow model. Built-in real-time OFAC screening, transaction monitoring, and risk scoring.

September 2025
Strategy

Bank Modernization Framework

FIS introduces "Client at the Core" bank modernization framework — a componentized, incremental modernization approach that allows banks to modernize without full rip-and-replace. Supports FIS core, third-party core, and coreless deployment modes.

January 12, 2026
Acquisition Closed

Issuer Solutions Acquisition Closed

FIS completes $13.5B acquisition of Global Payments Issuer Solutions. Branded as "FIS Total Issuing Solutions." Creates one of the world's largest credit card issuing platforms. Expands FIS total addressable market to $28B.
